## 1. Recruiter Reality

### What Entry Tier 1 SOC Recruiters Expect

Defense contractor technical recruiters screening for Tier 1 SOC roles are looking for fast answers to four questions. If your materials do not answer all four within 90 seconds, they move on.

| Question | What They Look For | How Your Materials Answer It |
|---|---|---|
| Can this person do the work? | Alert triage, SIEM familiarity, IR documentation, Windows Event IDs | FIM case study + detection library at hawkinsops.com |
| Do they have the baseline cert? | Security+ (DoD 8140 / DCWF 511 floor) | PRE: "exam scheduled [date]" / POST: "CompTIA Security+ [cert ID]" |
| Will clearance be a problem? | Eligible and willing; no derogatory flags implied | "Eligible to obtain; willing to pursue sponsorship" |
| Are they actually going to show up in Huntsville? | Commitment, not "planning to move someday" | "Available ASAP; onsite-ready; lease month-to-month after May 1" |

### What Defense Contractors Screen For First

| Screen Criterion | Weight | Your Position |
|---|---|---|
| Security+ or equivalent DoD 8140 baseline | HIGH — hard gate on many postings | PRE-EXAM: note date. POST-EXAM: front-load on every artifact. |
| DoD 8140 / DCWF awareness | MEDIUM — signals program fit | Know role 511, know the compliance framework, say it correctly |
| Documentation discipline | HIGH — non-negotiable on CSSP/SOC contracts | PROOF_PACK model, case study, reproducible artifacts demonstrate this directly |
| Shift readiness / onsite | MEDIUM–HIGH — 24/7 SOC operations norm | Explicitly confirm onsite availability and schedule flexibility |
| SIEM platform familiarity | HIGH — Wazuh counts; Splunk counts | Name both platforms by name with specific experience |
| Windows endpoint knowledge | HIGH — most alerts are Windows | Event IDs, Sysmon, Wazuh agent on Windows — all documented |
| Communication clarity | HIGH — T1 writes tickets, not essays | Keep all written outreach concise; one point per sentence |

### What NOT to Say

| Do Not Say | Why | Say Instead |
|---|---|---|
| "I need to be employed by April/May" | Signals desperation; shifts negotiating leverage | "I'm available ASAP and onsite-ready" |
| "My lease ends in May" | Personal detail that invites lowball offers | Say nothing. It is not the recruiter's business. |
| "I built 139 rules" (as the opener) | Sounds like a number, not a skill | "I can triage alerts, investigate incidents, and document findings. Here's proof." |
| "I'm basically a detection engineer" | Wrong role label for this target | "I'm targeting Tier 1 SOC. Detection work is in my background." |
| "I'm relocating for a September birthday deadline" | Bizarre in a professional context | Say nothing about September. Move timeline is your business. |
| "I've been building my lab for months" | Implies you've been unemployed building things | "I've been developing and validating security operations skills through a structured home lab." |
| "AI does a lot of the work for me" | Triggers red flags in defense context | "I use AI to structure investigation workflows; all triage decisions are mine." |
| "I don't have clearance yet" | Deficit framing | "I'm eligible to obtain clearance and willing to pursue sponsorship." |
| "I'm self-taught" | Invites questions about gaps | "I built hands-on skills through a structured lab environment" — then point to the proof. |
| Any mention of custody schedule, income pressure, or personal financial constraints | Completely off-limits in professional outreach | None of this enters a professional conversation. Ever. |

### Rule: Recruiter vs Manager vs PM Contact Order

```
Default order: TA Recruiter → SOC Manager (if no recruiter response in 7 days) → PM (only with strong context)

Exception 1: Warm intro from a known contact → go directly to that person regardless of title.
Exception 2: Small companies (< 50 employees, e.g., MAD Security, H2L Solutions) → no TA layer exists; email or apply directly to the hiring manager.
Exception 3: If a job posting lists a specific contact email or hiring manager name → use it directly.

Never: Contact a SOC Manager cold to ask "are you hiring?" without a referral or specific job posting context.
```

## 10. Availability + Relocation Script Pack

Use these exact phrasings. Do not improvise on these questions — improvisation produces desperation signals.

---

### "Are you local to Huntsville?"

```
I'm in North Alabama and commuting to Huntsville is straightforward. I'm committing to Huntsville by start date and available onsite on short notice.
```

*(If they push for specifics: "I'm in the process of transitioning my living situation to Huntsville and available to start before that move finalizes.")*

---

### "When can you start?"

```
I'm available to start as soon as an offer is in place. I don't have a conflicting employment obligation. If onboarding requires any lead time for clearance paperwork or badging, I can work around that.
```

*(If they need a hard date: "I can give you a firm date once we're at the offer stage, but I'm targeting the earliest possible start — no commitments holding me back.")*

---

### "Are you willing to relocate?"

```
Yes. I'm targeting Huntsville specifically — not open to any market, but committed to this one. I'm onsite-ready and will make the logistics work around the start date.
```

*(Do NOT say: "I'm hoping to be there by September." That is a soft deadline that signals you are not committed now.)*

---

### "Do you have clearance?"

```
I don't hold an active clearance. I'm eligible to obtain one and willing to pursue sponsorship. If this role requires sponsorship, I'm prepared to initiate the process on day one.
```

*(If they ask for more detail: "No adverse history, no foreign contacts of concern, no financial disqualifiers — the standard eligibility indicators are clean.")*

---

### "What is your relocation timeline?"

```
I can be onsite for interviews immediately. For a full relocation, I can be set up in Huntsville within 30 days of an accepted offer — less if needed.
```

*(Month-to-month lease gives you flexibility. Do NOT mention the lease. Do NOT mention May 1. Do NOT mention a birthday or a September goal. The only information the recruiter needs is: you can be there fast.)*

---

### "Are you interviewing elsewhere?"

```
I'm actively in process with a few other positions in the Huntsville area. I'm targeting this market specifically, and [Company] is a priority for me.
```

*(Even if you have no other interviews, this is an appropriate response — you are actively applying, which is the factual basis for it. It also signals you have alternatives without sounding desperate.)*

---

### "What are your salary expectations?"

```
I'm looking for market rate for a Tier 1 SOC Analyst in Huntsville — my understanding is that range runs roughly $65,000–$85,000 for a non-cleared role, higher with active clearance or sponsorship. I'm open to the range if the role and growth path are the right fit.
```

*(ZipRecruiter showed $70k–$135k for SOC Analyst in Huntsville; the lower end of that is realistic for an entry Tier 1 role. Do not lead with a number below $65k — it anchors the conversation low.)*
